PBD Journey — Building a Physics Engine from a Research Paper
A daily log of a 16-year-old teaching himself C++, mathematics, and physics by implementing Position Based Dynamics — a real-time simulation method used in games and film.

Starting from a 2006 reaserch paper by Müller et al., I am building a cloth simulator from scratch in C++. No tutorials. No courses. Just the paper, the math, and the code.
This repository documents every day of that process — what I understood, what broke, what I had to derive, and what finally clicked. It is not a polished project log. It is an honest record of what it actually looks like to go from beginner to building something real.
What I am building toward:
A real-time 3D cloth simulator with distance constraints, bending resistance, collision detection, and tearing — implemented from mathematical first principles in C++.
Why I am doing this publicly:
Because the process is the point. Anyone can post a finished project. This is the unfinished version — every confusion, every breakthrough, every line of code that finally compiles after two hours of debugging.
Because a year from now I want proof — to myself more than anyone else — that I did this.
